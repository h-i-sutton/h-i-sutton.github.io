![Flag](/images/flag_germany.jpg) Bonex, a German diver propulsion vehicle (DPV) manufacturer, is known to supply several Special Forces units including the German Kampfschwimmers and Turkish SAT (*SualtÄ± Taarruz*, translating as 'Underwater Offence').   

![Flag](/images/Bonex1.jpg)   

The Bonex HP DPV is in roughly the same class as the Italian Suex types, being a compact tow-behind scooter. The family crosses over from civilian to military market with a tougher finish and navigation add-ons for nighttime and poor visibility diving.   

![Flag](/images/Bonex2.jpg)   
The type has a 3.5mm carbon fiber tube with hard coated aluminum back end. The nosecone is Delrin and the propeller nozzle is also aluminum.   

![Flag](/images/Bonex3.jpg)   

 **Bonex HP Shuttle DPD**   

Bonex offers a small platform which allows two HP DPVs to be attached side by side with the diver laying on the frame in the middle, protected from the oncoming water by a simple Perspex watershield. This equates to other DPDs (Diver Propulsion Devices, in this sense used to denote a larger DPV) such as the Stidd and Suex twin-DPV rigs.   

![Flag](/images/BonexShuttle.jpg)   

**Specification**  

Length: 0.85m  

Beam: 1.050m   

Height: 0.35m   

Weight: 60kg   

Operating depth: 150m (subjet to diver's limits)   

Speed: 5kts (max) with single diver   

Range(Cruise Speed, sngle diver): 16nm (30km)   

Crew: 1 pilot and 1 passenger
