#Iranian Fateh Class Submarine Proposed With AIP![Flag Iran](/images/flag_iran.jpg) Iran has displayed a version of their first indigenous full-size submarine, the Fateh Class, with AIP (Air Independent Power). According to information [gathered by Naval News](https://www.navalnews.com/event-news/dimdex-2024/2024/03/iran-unveils-aip-version-of-fateh-class-submarine-at-dimdex-2024/), the Iranians plan to install the AIP on the first Fateh. If it is deemed successful, it could be installed on subsequent boats.   

The AIP is likely a Stirling engine type, or less likely, a fuel cell type. The model appears to show three Stirling generators in containers. The AIP is housed in a hull insert behind the sail and ahead of the main propulsion spaces approximately 5 meters long. The liquid oxygen (LOX) is contained in a number of smaller flasks in a false keel running along the bottom of the hull.  

![Iran-Fateh-Class-Submarine](/images/Iran-Fateh-AIP-Model.jpg)
