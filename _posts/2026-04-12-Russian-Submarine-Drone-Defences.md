![Flag Russia](/images/flag_russia.jpg) Two Russian Navy KILO Class submarines have been photographed in Kronstadt, the naval base in St Petersburg. The photo reveals details of their anti-drone (aerial drones – UAVs and surface drones – USVs) defences. 

The two boats [have been identified](https://bsky.app/profile/mrfrantarelli.bsky.social/post/3mjaofsheck2m) as the Pr. 06363 Improved KILO class submarine _Mozhaisk_ (B-608) and the older Pr. 877EKM KILO class submarine _Dmitrov_ (B-806).

The observed defences consist of a pedestal mounted heavy machine gun (possibly an NSV 12.7mm) on the aft casing behind the fin (sail), and a searchlight on navigation bridge. A second submarine behind has an anti-drone cage. There appears to be a second (unoccupied) pintle mount for a machinegun next to the searchlight on the nearest boat. This pintle appears to be occupied, possibly by a machinegun, in the second boat.

The submarines’ command likely feels that they are, even in the Baltic, vulnerable to Ukrainian drones. It is questionable how effective these defences would be.
