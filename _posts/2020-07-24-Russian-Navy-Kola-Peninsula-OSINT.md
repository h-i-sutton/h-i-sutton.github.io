**OSINT: Russian Navy Movements in Kola Peninsula, July 2020**   
![Flag](/images/flag_russia.jpg) A collage of Sentinel satellite imagery (from [Sentinel hub](https://apps.sentinel-hub.com/sentinel-playground/)) of the Russian Northern Fleet in the Kola Peninsula. ESA make available a range of imagery from their satellites including Sentinel-2. This is lower resolution than many other satellites but makes frequent, almost daily, passes of most locations of interest. Even though it is low resolution, a trained eye can identify submarines on the surface, and of course warships.   

OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) provided by Frank Bottema ([Twitter](https://twitter.com/FrankBottema)) and @MatchlessMan410 ([Twitter](https://twitter.com/MatchlessMan410)).   

*Original graphic. CLICK for high-resolution image.*  
[![](/images/Russia-submarines-Kola-peninsula-July2020-940.jpg)](/images/Russia-submarines-Kola-peninsula-July2020.jpg)   

Of particular interest, the GUGI special mission submarine [BS-64 Podmoskovye](http://www.hisutton.com{% post_url 2019-09-12-Russian_Submarine_BS-64_Payload %}) is seen leaving [Olenya Guba](http://www.hisutton.com{% post_url 2019-08-23-Ru_Olenya_Guba %}) on July 17, assisted by at least two tugs. She is seen again the next day on the surface in the Motovsky Gulf. In this image she appears to have a payload on her back.

There was a submarine rescue exercise in Motovsky Gulf around this time, involving the Pr.1855 Priz-class deep-submergence rescue vehicle AS-34. See photo below. It is unclear whether BS-64 was involved, but she can carry a DSRV on her back, so this may be the payload seen. Alternatively it may relate the Shelf seabed nuclear power plants, or a seabed sonar array system.
